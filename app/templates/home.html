Medical Info

A pulmonary embolism (PE) is a blood clot that develops in a blood vessel in the body (often in the leg). It then travels to a lung artery where it suddenly blocks blood flow. Blood clotting is a normal process to prevent bleeding. The body makes blood clots and then breaks them down. The body does not always break down a clot and this can lead to serious health issues. Slowed blood flow, an abnormality in clot forming, or an injury to the blood vessel wall are common causes of blood clots.

Artificial Intelligence

Using a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) based algorithm, the PE detector, which used primarily by radiologists, is responsible for identifying the presence of PE for CTPA scanned, hospitalized clients or patients.

The main benefit offered by the PE detector is that it addresses:
  • the issues of the unavailability of Radiologists in the medical facilities
  • the likeliness of human errors and the inefficient detection of PE, which can gravely affect patients.
